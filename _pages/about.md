I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Operations Research at Georgia Tech's [Physical Internet Center](https://www.picenter.gatech.edu/), advised by [Dr. Benoit Montreuil](https://www.isye.gatech.edu/users/benoit-montreuil). Before coming to Atlanta, I completed my B.Tech. in Mechanical Engineering (with Honors) at the [Indian Institute of Technology Madras](https://www.iitm.ac.in/) in 2016.

My research focuses on building smarter supply chain networks — particularly *hyperconnected* systems, where physical and digital flows are tightly integrated across many organizations. I'm interested in questions like: How do we detect demand disruptions before they cascade through a network? How can supply and demand be aligned in real time when production options shift dynamically? And how can autonomous technologies make the distribution of critical goods more resilient at scale?

To tackle these problems, I draw on a mix of **machine learning, simulation, and optimization**. Much of my work is grounded in real operational contexts — from improving logistics demand forecasting accuracy to redesigning how personal protective equipment was distributed during the COVID-19 pandemic at Georgia Tech.

I'm also interested in how supply chain education can be reimagined through realistic, data-driven gaming simulators that mirror the complexity of real-world systems.

If you're working on supply chain resilience, hyperconnected logistics, or data-driven operations research — feel free to reach out.
