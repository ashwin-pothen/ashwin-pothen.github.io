Societally critical products — those essential to public health, safety, and societal continuity — face unique supply chain challenges during crises, including sudden demand spikes and disrupted supply. This paper defines what constitutes a societally critical product and presents a conceptual framework for designing a hyperconnected and autonomous distribution system tailored to their needs. The framework covers physical infrastructure, organizational coordination, information flow, and operational protocols, providing a foundation for building more resilient distribution systems for critical goods.
